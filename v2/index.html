A Classic Italian Pasta Dish

Introduction

Fettuccine Alfredo is a beloved Italian dish known for its smooth texture and rich flavor. This comfort food has been enjoyed by my family for generations.

The dish originated in Rome in the early 20th century when restaurant owner Alfredo di Lelio created it to help his wife regain her strength after childbirth.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (450g) fettuccine pasta
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 2 cups (480ml) heavy cream
  • 2 cups (200g) freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta

    Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add salt generously. Add the fettuccine pasta and cook until al dente, usually 8-10 minutes.

  2. Start the Sauce

    While the pasta is cooking, melt the butter in a large, deep skillet over medium heat. If using garlic, add it to the melted butter and saut√© for about 30 seconds.

  3. Add Cream

    Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for about 2 minutes.

  4. Add Cheese

    Remove the skillet from heat and gradually whisk in the grated Parmesan cheese until melted and smooth. Season with black pepper and salt to taste.

  5. Combine with Pasta

    Add the drained fettuccine to the sauce and toss gently to coat.

  6. Serve

    Serve immediately in warm bowls. Garnish with additional Parmesan and chopped parsley if desired.

Tips for Perfect Alfredo

Use Fresh Ingredients

Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese rather than pre-grated options for better flavor.

Serve Immediately

Fettuccine Alfredo is best enjoyed immediately after preparation.
